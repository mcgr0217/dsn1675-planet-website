Moons

Mars moon 1

Phobos is a bit larger than Deimos, and orbits only 6,000 km (3,700 miles) above the Martian surface. No known moon orbits closer to its planet.

Mars moon 2

Deimos orbits Mars at a distance of about 23,500 km (about 14,100 mi), completing an orbit once every 1.26 Earth days. The moon’s orbit is almost circular and is only slightly tilted relative to the Martian equator.

Discovery

If viewed from the surface of Mars near its equator, full Phobos looks about one third as big as the Earth's full moon from Earth. It has an angular diameter of between 8' (rising) and 12' (overhead). It would look smaller when the observer is further away from the Martian equator, and is completely invisible (always beyond the horizon) from Mars' polar ice caps.

The names, originally spelled Phobus and Deimus, respectively, were suggested by Henry Madan (1838–1901)
